Elon Musk

Here's a time line of Dr.Borlaug's business career:

    Zip2

  • 1995 - founded Zip2 with his brother Kimbal, and Greg Kouri with $28,000 in funding.
  • February 1999 - received $22 million for his 7-percent share from Compaq that has acquired Zip2 for $307 million in cash.

    X.com and PayPal

  • 1999 - co-founded X.com, an online financial services and e-mail payment company.
  • 2000 - returned as CEO of the X.com that has merged with online bank Confinity.
  • September 2000 - replaced with Thiel.
  • 2001 - the company focused on the money-transfer service and was renamed PayPal.
  • 2002 - the largest shareholder with 11.72% of shares--received $175.8 million from eBay has acquired PayPal for $1.5 billion in stock.
  • 2017 - purchased the X.com domain from PayPal for its sentimental value.
  • 2022 - discussed a goal of creating "X, the everything app".

    SpaceX

  • 2001 - involved with the nonprofit Mars Society and discussed funding plans to place a growth-chamber for plants on Mars.
  • October 2001 - traveled to Moscow with Jim Cantrell and Adeo Ressi to buy refurbished intercontinental ballistic missles (ICBMs) that could send the greenhouse payloads into space.
  • February 2002 - returned to Russia with Mike Griffin to look for three ICBMs.
  • May 2002 - founded SpaceX and became the company's CEO and Chief Engineer.
  • 2006 - SpaceX attempted its first launch of the Falcon 1 rocket, but failed. Then, it was awarded a Commercial Orbital Transportation Services program contract from NASA Administrator and former SpaceX consultant Mike Griffin.
  • 2008 - SpaceX succeeded in launching the Falcon 1 into orbit and received a $1.6 billion Commercial Resupply Services contract from NASA for 12 flights of its Falcon 9 rocket and Dragon spacecraft to the International Space Station.
  • 2012 - credited the NASA award, one of the last actions by Mike Griffin as NASA Administrator, for saving the company.
  • 2015 - SpaceX successfully landed the first stage of a Falcon 9 on an inland platform.
  • 2018 - SpaceX launched the Falcon Heavy.
  • 2019 - SpaceX has been developing Starship to replace the Falcon 9 and the Falcon Heavy.
  • 2020 - SpaceX launched its first crewed flight, the Demo-2.

    Tesla

  • February 2004 - invested $6.5 million, became the majority shareholder, and joined Tesla's board of directors as chairman.
  • 2008 - assumed leadership of the company as CEO and product architect, and Tesla first built an eletric sports car, the Roadster.
  • 2009 - became Tesla co-founder, along with Tarpenning and two others.
  • 2012 - Tesla began delivery of its four-door Model S sedan.
  • 2015 - A crossover, the Model X was launched.
  • 2017 - A mass-market sedan, the Model 3, was released, and bestselling plug-in electric car worldwide.
  • 2019 - the longest-tenured CEO of any automotive manufacturer globally.
  • 2020 - A fifth vehicle, the Model Y crossover, was launched.
  • 2021 - nominally changed his title to "Technoking" while retaining his position as CEO.
  • November 2021 - proposed on Twitter to sell 10% of Tesla stock.
  • February 2022 - under investigation by the SEC for possible insider trading related to the sale.
  • 2022 - unveiled a robot developed by Tesla, Optimus.

    Twitter

  • 2017 - expressed interest in buying Twitter.
  • January 2022 - started purchasing Twitter shares.
  • March 2022 - reaching a 5% stake in the company.
  • April 2022 - owned a 9% stake, became the largest shareholder, agreed to a deal that would appoint him to Twitter's board of directors and prohibit him from acquiring more than 14.9% of the company, and made a $43 billion offer to buy Twitter, launching a takeover bid to buy 100% of Twitter's stock at $54.20 per share.
  • July 2022 - formally sued by Twitter in the Chancery Court of Delaware for breaching a legally binding agreement to purchase Twitter.
  • October 2022 - reversed again, offering to purchase Twitter at $54.20 per share and the acquisition was officially completed.
